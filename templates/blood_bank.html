Frequently Asked Questions

In general, to donate blood you must be:

  • In good health
  • At least 17 years old in most states (16 years old with parental consent in some states)
  • Weigh at least 110 pounds
  • Not have donated blood in the last 56 days

Some medical conditions or medications may disqualify you from donating blood. Check with your local blood bank for specific eligibility requirements.

You can donate whole blood every 56 days (about 8 weeks). Plasma can be donated more frequently, up to twice a week, while platelets can be donated every 7 days up to 24 times a year. Double red cell donations can only be made every 112 days.

The entire process takes about one hour from check-in to refreshments. The actual blood donation only takes about 8-10 minutes. The rest of the time is spent on registration, health history, and a brief physical examination.

Yes, donating blood is very safe. All equipment used for the collection is sterile and used only once. The average adult has about 10 pints of blood, and only 1 pint is taken during donation. Your body replaces the fluid within 24 hours and the red cells within a few weeks.
